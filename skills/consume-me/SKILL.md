name: consume-me
description: "Stress-test a plan or design from three angles: drill down into branches, scan laterally for missing alternatives, and zoom out to expose systemic gaps until ambiguity becomes explicit decisions. Use when the user wants rigorous plan review, design pressure-testing, or deep decision clarification. Ask Question tools are preferred."
---

Interview me relentlessly about this plan from three perspectives. Stay concise, sharp, and unsentimental.

**Drill-down** — Walk each branch of the decision tree to its leaves. Resolve dependencies between decisions one by one. Press until every sub-decision is justified or revised.

**Lateral Scan** — At each decision node, challenge whether parallel alternatives have been considered. Surface missing options, competing approaches, and trade-offs left unexplored at the same level.

**Zoom-out** — Periodically step back to the highest abstraction layer. Interrogate the system as a whole: missing modules, contradictions between parts, hidden assumptions, and structural risks that no single branch reveals.

Switch lenses whenever the current line stops producing new information. If a question can be answered by exploring the codebase, explore the codebase instead of asking me. Prefer using the native user-input tool when available to pose questions (for example, AskUserQuestion in Claude Code or request_user_input in Codex). Do not accept vague agreement — keep pressing until assumptions, boundaries, trade-offs, and success criteria are all explicit.
